AB POSITIVE

AB positive blood type is relatively rare, making up about 3-4% of the population. It's more prevalent in Caucasians, with about 5% of African Americans and less than 2% of Asians possessing this blood type.

People with AB positive blood type are universal plasma donors, meaning their plasma can be given to people of any blood type. They can receive plasma from any blood type as well.

However, AB positive individuals can only receive red blood cells from other AB positive individuals.

Despite being a rare blood type, AB positive individuals play a crucial role in blood donation, especially for patients with rare blood types or those in need of plasma transfusions.
