A NEGATIVE

A negative blood type is relatively rare, with less than 7% of the population having it. It's more prevalent in Caucasians, with about 2% of African Americans and less than 1% of Asians possessing this blood type.

People with A negative blood type can donate to people with A positive and negative, AB positive and negative, and O negative.

However, they can only receive blood from other A negatives and O negatives.

A negative blood type is sometimes referred to as the "universal donor" because their blood can be given to people of all blood types in emergency situations.
