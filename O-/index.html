O NEGATIVE

O negative blood type is relatively rare, making up about 6-7% of the population. It's more prevalent in Caucasians, with about 9% of African Americans and less than 4% of Asians possessing this blood type.

People with O negative blood type are universal donors, meaning their blood can be given to people of any blood type. However, they can only receive blood from other O negative individuals.

Despite being a rare blood type, O negative individuals play a crucial role in blood donation, especially in emergency situations when the recipient's blood type is unknown.

O negative blood type is often in high demand by hospitals and blood banks due to its ability to be used in emergencies and for newborns with unknown blood types.
