A POSITIVE

A positive blood type is the second most common blood type, with about 28% of the population having it. It's more prevalent in Caucasians, with about 30% of African Americans and less than 27% of Asians possessing this blood type.

People with A positive blood type can donate to people with A positive and AB positive, and can receive blood from people with A and O blood types.

However, they cannot receive blood from people with B or AB negative blood types.

A positive blood type is sometimes referred to as the "universal recipient" because they can receive blood from all types except for A and O negative.
