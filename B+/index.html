B POSITIVE

B positive blood type is the third most common blood type, making up about 9-11% of the population. It's more prevalent in Caucasians, with about 10% of African Americans and less than 8% of Asians possessing this blood type.

People with B positive blood type can donate to people with B positive and AB positive blood types. They can receive blood from people with B and O blood types.

However, they cannot receive blood from people with A or AB negative blood types due to the presence of anti-A antibodies in their blood.

Despite being a common blood type, B positive individuals play a crucial role in blood donation, especially for patients with B or AB blood types.
