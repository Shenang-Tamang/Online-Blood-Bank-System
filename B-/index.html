B NEGATIVE

B negative blood type is relatively rare, making up about 1-2% of the population. It's more prevalent in Caucasians, with about 2% of African Americans and less than 1% of Asians possessing this blood type.

People with B negative blood type can donate to other B negative individuals, as well as AB negative individuals. They can receive blood from B negative and O negative individuals.

However, they cannot receive blood from B positive individuals due to the presence of anti-B antibodies in their blood.

Despite being a rare blood type, B negative individuals play a crucial role in blood donation, especially for patients with rare blood types or those in need of plasma transfusions.
